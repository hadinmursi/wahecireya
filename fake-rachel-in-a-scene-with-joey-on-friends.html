Friends Had A Different Approach When It Came To Shooting Episodes

Friends had a tendency of doing things differently. To the show's credit, the formula clearly worked, as the sitcom thrived for ten seasons and will continue to do so for many more years, thanks to their syndication rights.

One of the things the show did differently, was their ability to listen to the audience. If the crowd did react in a certain way, they were willing to change the line on the fly, something that is a big time 'no no' for other sitcoms.

Lisa Kudrow asking the audience if they understood a joke while shooting the Vegas episode is a classic example.

Friends Had Lots Of Other Scenes That Didn't Make The Final Cut

There were several scenes that came close to airing, but ultimately did not make it to the final cut. One of them, included Monica and Chandler at the airport for their honeymoon, getting stopped for using the word 'bomb'. It was sensitive moment at the time, with 9-11 taking place. This caused the Friends creators to ultimately remove the scene and use it as a deleted scene extra on the DVD.

Another moment in particular, was canceled altogether and didn't even make it to production. Alongside Andy Cohen, Matthew Perry recalled instantly turning down a storyline, which seemed for suitable for Joey given that it involved sandwiches.

He recalls, "There was a story line on 'Friends' where Chandler went to a male strip joint because he really liked the sandwiches," Perry recalled. "And I called up and I said, 'Let's not do this one.'"

Perry got his way and in truth, the show used a lot of his creative output for Chandler along the way, including the unique way he was able to deliver his lines.
